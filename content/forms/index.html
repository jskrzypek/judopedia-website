There are many forms in Judo which are most commonly known as Kata (形). It can also be written with the kanji (型) however this is an older way of writing it.[1]

Formal Kata

As defined by the Kōdōkan Judo Institute.[2]

Romanization Translation
Nage-no-kata Throwing forms
Katame-no-kata Grappling forms
Kime-no-kata Combat forms
Jū-no-kata Forms of gentleness
Kōdōkan-goshin-jutsu Kōdōkan self-defense forms
Itsutsu-no-kata Five forms
Ko-shiki-no-kata Classical/antique forms
Seiryoku-zen'yō-kokumin-taiiku Maximum-efficiency national physical education

Other Kata

These are either rarely practiced or were not developed by the Kodokan.

Romanization Translation
Joshi-jūdō-goshin-hō Jūdō directives of self-defence for girls
Go-no-kata Forms of hardness
Kaeshi-no-kata Reversal forms
Renkoho Arresting Techniques
Kime-shiki Forms of decision
Go-no-sen-no-kata Forms of counter attack
Shobu-no-kata Forms of Contest
Nage-ura-no-kata Throwing counter forms
