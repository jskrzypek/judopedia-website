Note: In the Judo world, the techniques are generally called by their Japanese name. This site uses the Kōdōkan Technique Classification as a guide for the naming of the techniques using the Revised Hepburn romanization system to translate the words using Roman characters. There are variations as well as completely different classifications, some of which can be found on the Classification of Techniques page.

Basic Techniques

Sitting

There are a couple acceptable ways to sit that are acceptable in all Judo clubs / dojos. Some places may be more relaxed about this than others.

Romanization Translation
Anza Sitting cross-legged
Seiza Formal sitting position

Posture

Good posture gives a well centered balance and is vital for techniques to be executed properly.

Romanization Translation
Shizen-hontai Basic natural posture
Shizen-tai Natural posture
Jigo-hontai Basic defensive posture
Jigo-tai Defensive posture

Breakfalls

Known as Ukemi.

A breakfall is a way to protect the body by distributing the force of the fall. This is something everyone that practices Judo must master as you will get thrown. A lot.

Romanization Translation
Ushiro Ukemi Rear breakfall
Yoko Ukemi Side breakfall
Mae Mawari Ukemi Forward rolling breakfall
Mae Ukemi Forward breakfall

Kōdōkan Classification of Techniques

For the purposes of this website, techniques will be classified by the Kōdōkan Technique Classification. The Kōdōkan classification only classifies techniques which are considered decisive win. Other techniques are practiced, they just do not have official names. These techniques are still considered Judo as long as it helps get you closer to a decisive win and does not go against the principles of Judo.

Other classifications, such as the International Judo Federation's, can be found on the Classification of Techniques page.

Note: Basically there are two types of constriction techniques. Generally, strangle is considered to block the flow of blood while a choke will block the flow of air. Some people call these Blood Choke and Air Strangle to make it more apparent. However, due to the ambiguity between these two words, they are often used interchangeably.
